## The problem: copy-paste drift

Most teams share CI quality and compliance checks by forking or copying YAML/scripts into each repo. Within weeks, repos diverge: one team upgrades the compliance audit, another hardens the quality thresholds — and there is no single source of truth. Re-syncing means manually diffing across repos and hoping nothing important slipped through.

## The shadcn model

repo-harness borrows the pattern that [shadcn/ui](https://ui.shadcn.com) uses for UI components: **vendor the code into your repo** rather than hiding it behind a package boundary.

Every gate is split into two layers:

| Layer | Owner | Update behaviour |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| **Managed engine** (workflows, bot scripts) | repo-harness | Overwritten on `update` if file is unedited; otherwise placed as `.harness-new` for manual merge |
| **Owned policy** (`config.mjs`, `controls.mjs`, `QA-MEMORY.md`) | Your team | Scaffolded once, **never overwritten** — your policy lives in your repo |

This means you get upstream engine improvements with a single command while keeping full control over your thresholds, allowlists, and compliance controls.

## Quickstart

```bash
# 1. Initialise the manifest in your repo
npx repo-harness init

# 2. Add the gates you want
npx repo-harness add quality compliance

# 3. Check what's installed
npx repo-harness list
```

After `init`, a `repo-harness.json` manifest is created at your repo root. After `add`, the managed engine files are vendored into `.github/` and `scripts/`, and the owned policy stubs are scaffolded — ready for you to fill in.
